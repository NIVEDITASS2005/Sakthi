About Tamil Nadu

Tamil Nadu is the 11th largest state of India and located in the southernmost part of the country. Its capital city is Chennai. One can explore the different fascinating regions in this state including beautiful white sand beaches, the Nilgiri Hills, tea gardens and numerous architectural wonders spread all across. One can find several cheap yet good hotels located throughout the state that offer excellent stays to travellers.

Tamil Nadu has a lot to offer to all kinds of travellers, irrespective of their age groups. Family vacations are as fun as the experiences for backpackers in this gorgeous South Indian state.

Top cities and localities of Tamil Nadu

  • Chennai: It is the capital city of the state and also the biggest city area wise. It is one of the five major metropolitan cities of the country. Other than being an IT hub, the city has quite a number of sightseeing options for tourists, which is why it has been ranked among the top 100 tourist-friendly cities of the world. One can easily spot several budget hotels within the city perimeter.
  • Coimbatore: It is the second largest city of the state. The Western Ghats on the western side of the city give it a majestic look. The green pastures here make the city a favourite among nature seekers. You can also find outstanding deals and offers on hotels here.
  • Coonoor: This is a small town located amid the Nilgiris with tea gardens all around. The weather here is exceptionally inviting for tourists and one can experience nature at its best while staying at the excellent luxury hotels located here such as the Taj Gateway.
  • Madurai: This city is also famously known as the ‘city of temples’ and one can find several architectural marvels here. Different dynasties which ruled the state in earlier centuries have left their mark in the form of temples here.

Things to do

  • Meenakshi Amman Temple: This is an excellent example of South Indian temple architecture. The carvings are eye-catching with bright colours and exceptional clarity. People come to visit this temple from far and wide as it is one of its kind.
  • Nataraja Temple: This Shiva temple is another fine example of the architecture from earlier centuries. It has a huge following among Hindus and people flock to the temple to get their wishes fulfilled by the blessings of God.
  • Five Rathas: There are five rathas here that are said to have been carved from a single rock. Located in the south of Mamallapuram, every temple here is dedicated to one of the Hindu Gods. The architectural beauty is simply mind-boggling. One just can't keep one’s eyes off the carvings!
  • Marina Beach: It is one of the most famous beaches of the country, located in Chennai. One can spot several families and groups of friends flocking to the beach during the evenings, especially on weekends.
  • Covelong Beach: This beach is located on the outskirts of Chennai and offers a much quieter as well as a cleaner experience to visitors.
  • Arignar Anna Zoological Park: It is the oldest zoo of the country and has been relocated to the outskirts of Chennai. It is home to several endangered species of wildlife and they are being rehabilitated here too. You can book resorts at discounted prices around this park and visit other nearby places as well.

How to reach

  • By Air: There are international airports at Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai and Tiruchirappalli. Several other cities have domestic airports for enhanced connectivity. Most of the countries have flights coming in to these international airports.
  • By Rail: Almost all the cities in Tamil Nadu are connected through the railways with other major cities of the country.
  • By Road: Extensive road connectivity is available. One can either drive down or travel by the state or private-run bus network.

Transportation hubs

  • Cab: One can hire cabs or rent self-driven cars to explore the countryside.
  • Bus: The local and inter-city bus network is extremely dependable for all tourists.
  • Train: One can also opt for the railways while travelling from one city to another, especially in case there is shortage of time.

Attractions

Kanchipuram, Mahabalipuram, Fort St. George, Dakshinachitra, St. Mary’s Church, Padmanabhapuram Palace, Arunachaleshwar Temple, Shore Temple, Royal Palace and Rock Fort Temple are among its many attractions. There are several other places that one can visit in Tamil Nadu, so get started by booking the best hotels in the state at excellent discounts.
